INTRODUCTION

Hello!
My name is Alexander Cormier, and I go by Xander. I am a junior in Las Lomas High School, and I live in Walnut Creek. I have an interest in computer science, music, and math. Aside from my various hobbies, I love to spend time working on my academics, with my family, or in my community!
I have been on my schools' honor roll since 6th grade, while also juggling homework with my life outside of school. I am in boys team charity, which is a group of kids in grades 8-12 that volunteer in various places around the county. I am a part of the quiz bowl club in my school, which is a club based around academic competition with other people and schools.

ABOUT ME

Since I was a kid, I was always interested in things like video games and computers. Soon, I developed a desire to start making, instead of playing them. I took a class on coding when I was in 8th grade, which really sparked my intrigue for them. Now, I am learning more about it than ever before, and I am inspired to go into college studying coding and video games.
Cello is another passion of mine, in which I started when I was five. Over the last 10 (almost eleven!) years I learnt many new and interesting songs and techniques, and even dabbled in arranging music! I am part of both the Contra Costa Chamber Orchestra, the Center For Community Arts Cello Ensemble, which is a group of cello players that are all in high school and preform at various concerts and events, and I also am the first chair cellist in my school's orchestra. Other groups I am a part of are the The Young People's Symphony Orchestra, and the East Bay Chamber Orchestra.

My cello teacher is Stephen Evens, who has a BA in Cello Preformance and a MA in Orchestra Performance. He leads my Cello Ensemble, and is a great mentor for all his students. He can be contacted at stephen.evans29@gmail.com.
